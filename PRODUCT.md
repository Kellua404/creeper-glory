# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Developers, designers, and recruiters reviewing a portfolio. They visit once, scroll through in 2-4 minutes, and leave with an impression of the creator's frontend skill, taste, and technical ambition. Secondary: Minecraft fans who find the link shared socially.

## Product Purpose

A single-page animated landing page celebrating the Minecraft Creeper, built as Portfolio Project #1. The site exists to demonstrate advanced frontend craft: React component architecture, production-grade motion design, and technical depth. Success means a visitor says "this person knows what they're doing."

## Brand Personality

Dramatic. Pixel-precise. Quietly menacing.

The Creeper is a cultural icon: born of glitch, silent before destruction. The brand voice mirrors it — measured, lore-heavy, never loud, then suddenly explosive. Think: a Minecraft loading screen that became a film title card.

## Anti-references

- Corporate SaaS landing pages (gradients on white, "Supercharge your workflow")
- Pastel game fan sites (candy-colored, chibi icons, bubbly fonts)
- Generic "dark portfolio" templates (navy/purple, code screenshots, hero text saying "Full-Stack Developer")

## Design Principles

1. **Pixel fidelity first.** The aesthetic is pixel art and voxels. Every visual element earns its place by respecting the grid.
2. **Silence before the storm.** Restraint until the moment demands explosion. Motion is meaningful, not decorative reflex.
3. **The world is alive.** The Creeper lives in a world; the page should feel like a window into that world, not a poster about it.
4. **Craft is the message.** This is a portfolio piece. Technical ambition IS the content. Extraordinary effects aren't extras; they're the point.
5. **Faithful to the source.** Every design decision should be justifiable by reference to Minecraft lore, texture packs, or game mechanics.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA target
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected throughout (already implemented via `useReducedMotion` hook)
- Semantic HTML, ARIA labels on interactive elements
- Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements
